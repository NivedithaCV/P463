Exoplanet statistics

The statistical distribution of exoplanets helps us in mapping the trend in the planetary system and combined with the information of the host star, it helps develop and put constraints on the theory of planet formation and evolution. The study of planetary frequency in various parameter space has been done,comparing their mass, orbit orientation, orbital period, host star etc. The data samples that are used depends on the selection efficiency, the signal to noise ratio and the transit probability, these causes observational bias in the field In this article I have reviewed and presented the results from some of the relevant papers in the field. It is seen that statistical analysis could bring constraints and changes in the theories of planet formation. There is still research needed to be done in proving the theory to be valid for all the planetary systems. With the development in the spectroscopic survey of the exoplanets and the increase in sample with new missions more robust statistical analysis could be done to form models and theories in the exoplanet distribution and their understanding.
